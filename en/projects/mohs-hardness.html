Mohs Hardness Regression

Data science project predicting Mohs hardness of a mineral, given its properties.
TensorFlow, LightGBM, XGBoost, Scikit-Learn, Matplotlib, Seaborn

This project was done in the context of a Kaggle competion. The objective was to train a machine learning model to predict the hardness of a mineral on the basis of 11 different molecular properties. The metric used in order to score the participants was the Median Absolute Error. I performed an exploratory data analysis with some visualizations and then proceeded to train and evaluate various models: both shallow algorithms and deep neural networks in combination with ensemble methods such as voting and stacking. Perhaps unsurprisingly, the best models by far were stacked deep neural nets, i. e. neural nets which were trained using the original data plus the predictions of a another ML model.
