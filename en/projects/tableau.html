Tableau

Data visualization with interactive Dashboards

Good visualizations can be of great help when trying to communicate insights derived from data. Even though I had already gained extensive experience with three Python libraries for data visualization within the context of my Data Science Bootcamp (Matplotlib, Seaborn, and Bokeh), I thought it might be useful to also acquire a basic understanding of Tableau. As it is specifically designed for creating beautiful and easily manipulable dashboards, Tableau offers a multitude of possibilities and is frankly more intuitive to use than any Python framework.


The dashboard shown above visualizes the evolution of the Consumer Price Index (CPI) of airfares. Instead of having it display the raw data, one can also switch to a year-over-year or month-over-month comparative view. Additionally, the time period under consideration can be further narrowed down using the slider on the left. This way, one can zoom into the last 10 years, for example, to get a more detailed picture of how airfares were influenced by the course of the Covid pandemic. More dashboards can be found on my public Tableau profile.
