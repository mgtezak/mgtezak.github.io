Smoker Status Prediction

Data science project predicting smoker status using bio-signals.
LightGBM, XGBoost, Scikit-Learn, Matplotlib, Seaborn

This project was done in the context of a Kaggle competion. The objective was to train a machine learning model to predict whether or not a patient is currently a smoker, based on 22 numeric bio-markers. The metric used in order to score the participants of the competition was the area under the ROC curve.

I perform a fairly in-depth exploratory data analysis with many visualizations and then proceed to train and evaluate 8 different types of models ranging from a simple logistic regression up to a light gradient boosting classifier. Then I use ensemble methods such as voting and stacking, to further improve upon the base models.

Counterintuitively I found that the best predictor for being a smoker was body height, not because tallness itself causes an individual to smoke, but because being tall functions as a proxy for being male and men smoke more than women.
